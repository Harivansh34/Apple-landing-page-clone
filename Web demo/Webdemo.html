Biography

Linus Torvalds is a Finnish-Ameerican software engineer who is creator and the principal developer of Linux Kernel, which is a kernal for a number of operating systems. He also created the version controll system Git. Torvalds was born on December 28, 1969 in Helsinki, Finland. He studied computer science at University of Helsinki, and he developed Linux Kernel as a student project in 1991. Since then the Linux Kernal has became one of the most used kernels in the world, powering everything from smartphones to supercomputers.

Achievements

  • 1991- Created a Linux Keernal and version control system Git.
  • 2004- Named one of "100 Most Influential People in the World" by time magzine in 2004.
  • 2010- Recieved a honerary Doctrate from the University of Helsinki in 2010.
  • 2010- Recieved 2010 Mellinium Technology Prize and 2012 Free Software Foundation's Award for advancement of Free Software.
  • 2013- Inducted into the Internet Hall of Frame in 2013 for his contributions to the development Linux Kernel and creation of Git.
  • 2014- Inducted into Hall of Frame of the Computer History Museum in 2014.
  • 2015- Recieved the 2015 IEEE Computer Society Pioneer Award.
  • 2019- Recieved in 2019 ACM SIGOPS Hall of Frame Award.
  • 2021- Awarded the Takeda Foundation's 2021 Takeda Award for contributions, which recognizes individuals who have significant contributions to society through technology.
  • 2021- Recieved the 2021 IEEE Masaru Inbuka Consumer Electronics Award, which recognizes individuals who have significant contributions to the field of Consumer Electronics.
